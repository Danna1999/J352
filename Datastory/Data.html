The Navient Lawsuits: Report from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

Source: Student Loan Planner | The Navient Lawsuits: Large Settlement for Some, No Relief for Others by Clint Proctor

By Danna Matheus on Nov. 25, 2024

Navient Corporation, one of the biggest companies that manage federal student loans, has been involved in lawsuits because of how it handled borrower accounts. Many people have complained about how the company reported credit, handled credit repair, and managed other personal financial issues. According to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), there were 99 credit reports, credit repair services, or other personal consumer reports in January 2022, rising to 112 in January 2023. However, there were none in January 2024.

In January 2022, a settlement was reached with 39 state attorneys general, and Navient agreed to provide $1.85 billion in relief to borrowers, according to Student Loan Planner. This included canceling loans and offering restitution payments. The impact of the settlement was different from state to state. For example, California had 103,978 complaints, showing a larger number of affected borrowers, while Maryland had 21,921 complaints.

Navient insists it did nothing wrong and disagrees with the claims in the lawsuits, but it has agreed to make changes as part of the settlement. These changes include better training for staff on repayment options and stopping the practice of rewarding employees for handling calls quickly. Borrowers who qualify for relief or payments don't need to do anything as the payments and loan cancellations will happen automatically. Despite these changes, Navient's past mistakes have raised concerns about whether other federal student loan servicers are doing enough to help borrowers.
